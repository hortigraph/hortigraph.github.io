<p>2 OF 10) <strong>STATE</strong> THE <strong>FUNCTION</strong> OF: <code>CELL WALL</code></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Controls the shape</strong> of the cell</li>
<li>Limits cell expansion</li>
</ul>
<p>3 OF 10) <strong>STATE</strong> THE <strong>FUNCTION</strong> OF: <code>CELL MEMBRANE</code></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Controls movement</strong> of substances <strong>in and out</strong> of the cell (it is <code>SELECTIVELY PERMEABLE</code>)</li>
</ul>
<p>4 OF 10) <strong>STATE</strong> THE <strong>FUNCTION</strong> OF: <code>NUCLEUS</code></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Contains genetic information</strong> so <strong>controls cell processes</strong></li>
</ul>
<p>5 OF 10) <strong>STATE</strong> THE <strong>FUNCTION</strong> OF: <code>VACUOLE</code></p>
<ul>
<li>Controls cell <code>TURGOR</code></li>
<li>Stores waste products</li>
</ul>
<p>6 OF 10) <strong>STATE</strong> THE <strong>FUNCTION</strong> OF: <code>CYTOPLASM</code></p>
<ul>
<li>Enables <strong>substances</strong> to <strong>transported within</strong> the cell</li>
</ul>
<p>7 OF 10) <strong>STATE</strong> THE <strong>FUNCTION</strong> OF: <code>CHLOROPLAST</code></p>
<ul>
<li>The <strong>SITE OF PHOTOSYNTHESIS</strong> in the cell</li>
<li><strong>Contains</strong> <code>CHLOROPHYLL</code> used as a <code>CATALYST</code> in <code>PHOTOSYNTHESIS</code></li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li>Perform <code>RESPIRATION</code> to <strong>release energy</strong></li>
</ul>

</li>

<li>The <code>CAMBIUM</code> layer in <code>VASCULAR BUNDLES</code></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
