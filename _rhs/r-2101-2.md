<p><strong>1</strong> OF 11) <strong>STATE</strong> WHAT IS <strong>MEANT BY</strong> THE <strong>TERM</strong> “<code>PLANT TISSUE</code>”</p>
<ul>
<li><code>PLANT TISSUES</code> are <strong>GROUPS OF</strong> <code>DIFFERENTIATED CELLS</code> forming <strong>LAYERS WITH SPECIALISED FUNCTIONS</strong></li>
<li>Can be formed from ONE OR MORE TYPES OF CELL</li>
<li><code>PRIMARY TISSUE</code>  results from growth at the <code>APICAL MERISTEMS</code> (stems and roots)</li>
<li><code>SECONDARY TISSUE</code> result form growth at the <code>LATERAL MERISTEMS</code> (<code>CAMBIUM</code> in stems and roots)
<ul>
<li>Results in <code>SECONDARY THICKENING</code> in WOODY <code>PLANTS</code></li>
<li>Secondary growth also occurs in many nonwoody plants, e.g. <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tomato" title="Tomato">tomato</a>,<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secondary_growth#cite_note-1">[1]</a>  <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potato" title="Potato">potato</a>  <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tuber" title="Tuber">tuber</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carrot" title="Carrot">carrot</a>  <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taproot" title="Taproot">taproot</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sweet_potato" title="Sweet potato">sweet potato</a>  <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tuberous_root" title="Tuberous root">tuberous root</a>.</li>
<li>A few long-lived leaves also have secondary growth</li>
<li></li>
</ul>
